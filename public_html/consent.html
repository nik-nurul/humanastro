			What is this study about?
				- The human visual system is regularly proposed as the Gold standard for novel visual discovery in  astronomy, but with limited research to support the claim. As data collection volume grows, so does our dependance on Artificial intelligence and Machine Learning. So much so, technology is expected to make discoveries for astronomers. 
				With this in mind, this study attempts to answer the question "Are machines better than humans (at astronomy)?"

			Why am i being invited to participate?
				- You have been invited to particpate in this project because as an astronomer you have first-hand experience in astronomical discovery and we are interested to see how your experience and studies performs against the current standard of of technology in novel discovery.

			What's involved if i take part?
				- If you decide to participate, you will undertake several online timed tests on your personal computer. These tests are composed of several sequentially displayed images in which you must analyse and discover certain traits within the image. This will be done by an eye and cursor tracking program that records where you are looking and and pointing your cursor.
				With your permission, we would like to record the results of where you were looking and pointing in the form of heatmaps and whether or not you succesfully discovered the specific items. Any video or audio of you undertaking the test will not be recorded.
				You will be given the opportunity to leave feedback at all stages of the process.

			Possible benefits and risks of participating
				- We will not be asking for any personally identifying information, instead only recording, with your consent, non identifying audience demogrophic information. Your participation in this project will help the Astronomy industry better understand the usefulness of Artifiicial Intelligence and Machine Learning and create a more efficient process of scientific discovery.
				While we do not anticipate there is any risk of causing you distress, if there is at any point you do no wish to continue, you retain the right to do so.
				This test may display a flash of images thus participants with epilepsy should take precaution.
